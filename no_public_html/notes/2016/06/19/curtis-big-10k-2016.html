I will be running the Leicester Big 10k on the 17th of July for charity!

Three years ago I completed my Bronze Duke of Edinburgh Award, which involves volunteering for six months, learning to cook, camp and keep fit for three months and going on two multi-day walks around the countryside (camping included). After walking through fields with bulls, seeing a friend's tent collapse while camping in snow, and getting 3 hours sleep a night I decided to not continue onto the Silver Award. You'd never guess why... Now this year they are offering a special Diamond Award, and to earn it I get to do my favourite hobby while raising money for young people who really need it!

DofE is a charity that has existed for over 70 years and has inspired and transformed the lives of millions of young people from all walks of life. From volunteering to physical activities, life skills to expeditions, achieving a DofE Award is a passport to a brighter future, valued by employers and universities. It is the world's leading youth achievement award, and has support many disadvantaged young people across the UK and the world. It is aiming to give 2 million more people from the UK it's life-changing opportunities by 2020, and money raised from this run will go directly towards that.

Please consider donating, it will help so much! And thanks to all the support from everyone I've spoken to (or those who have donated) so far.
