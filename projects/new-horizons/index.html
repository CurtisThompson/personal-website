New Horizons

DateSeptember 2019 - May 2020
RoleProject Manager, Chief Tester and Evaluator
TechnologiesC#, Unity Game Engine, Python
DescriptionAn educational video game designed to teach KS3 Computer Science topics through a series of fun game-like puzzles. The New Horizons game adapts to the prior experiences of the player to provide puzzles of appropriate difficulty, and to give each player a unique playing experience.
Visit Website

Features

  • Five puzzles based on number representation, data representation, and logic
  • Each puzzle changes based on user experiences with adaptive learning techniques
  • Apache web-hosting and ElasticSearch data storage for remote access to the game
  • Overworld map allows choice of puzzles to play
  • 86% of students said they would like to see a game like this used in lessons

Questions

What was your role in the development of New Horizons?
My main role was as project manager of a team of six. As project manager I led the weekly team meetings, planned the progress of the team to ensure we met set deadlines, and communicated progress with the customer and supervisor. As Chief Tester and Evaluator I performed various forms of testing and planned the final study evaluation (which included gaining ethical approval from the university).
How did you evaluate the success of the game?
The success of the game was evaluated by performing a study in a local school. Students were asked to play the game for up to half an hour, then their Computer Science knowledge was compared before and after playing the game. Due to the short timeframe of the study no statistical significance was found, however it would be expected for a longer study. Study questionnaires showed that 86% of students would like to see a game like this used in their school lessons, and 67% belived their knowledge increased during the 30 minutes of gameplay.
How did you ensure that the data collection was done in an ethical manner?
To ensure data was collected in an ethical manner we decided to gain ethical approval from one of the university's ethics committees. This involved planning the study ahead of time with the participant's concerns in mind. We had to make sure that software used complied with GDPR, as well as ensure that participants were aware of the data and interaction involved. We successfully gained ethical approval before beginning the study, which meant that we were confident that we had adequate measures in place.
In what ways did you test the game prior to final evaluation?
Unit testing was performed within the Unity Engine on each developed puzzle. The results were fed back to the development team so that they could make necessary changes. User testing was also done in the form of 30-90 minute sessions with participants who were unfamiliar with the topics taught in the game to see how users would interact, providing valuable feedback to the development team. All of this testing was performed prior to the final study.
What skills did you learn or improve during this project?
As this was a large group project it allowed me to develop some skills that could not be done with an individual project. We extensively used Git in our development for version control, and I was able to improve my Git skills in this project. Since I was project manager, I was also able to develop soft skills such as instructing other team members as well as long-term planning. These soft skills were also applied to the testing section as I had to hold hour-long one-to-one sessions with individuals to assess how new users used our developed game.
Was the project a success?
Our group achieved 13 of the 19 initial requirements as agreed with our project customer. The project customer and project supervisor were also satisfied with the final game that we developed. With regards to the study, we found that 86% of students would like to see a game like this used in their school lessons, and 67% belived their knowledge increased during the 30 minutes of gameplay. Although no statistical significance in test scores was found due to the limited size of the study, the results were positive enough to suggest that a larger study may be able to find significant improvement.
