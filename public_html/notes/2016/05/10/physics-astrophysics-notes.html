Astrophysics Notes

Curtis Thompson Tuesday 10th May 2016 Academics

Astrophysics is the one area of physics which seems to get everyone excited no matter how much or how little they know about it. That's exactly why I chose this physics module to study - that and there are no options that particularly linked well to Computer Science. These hand-written notes are for the AQA specification of A2 Physics, specifically for the optional Astrophysics module. I have put many hours into making these notes for myself, but since I am told my notes are good I have made them available for you to download.

Click here to view and download the notes. (4MB)

The topics covered in the notes are:

  • Lenses
  • Refracting Telescopes
  • Reflecting Telescopes
  • Resolving Power
  • Charge-Coupled Devices
  • Non-Optical Telescopes
  • Apparent and Absolute Star Magnitude
  • Black Body Radiation
  • Star Classification
  • Hertzsprung-Russell Diagrams
  • Stellar Evolution
  • Supernovae
  • Doppler Effect
  • Hubble's Law
  • Evidence For The Big Bang
  • Quasars

I hope you find the notes useful. As of writing I have four more modules left to upload notes for; C4 and FP3 Mathematics, PHYA4 and PHYA5 Physics. Hopefully I will be able to upload these soon, but for now, enjoy these notes!
