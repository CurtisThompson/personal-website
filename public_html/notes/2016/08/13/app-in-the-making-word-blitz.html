App In The Making: Word Blitz

Curtis Thompson Saturday 13th August 2016 Projects and Programming

Over the past few days, when I've not been watching shows at the Fringe Festival, I've been dusting off my C# hat and working on a new app which will become my first published app on the Google Play Store. The name is 'Word Blitz' and in this article I will just share a few things about it for my eager readers.

Screenshot of the app in it's current development stage.

The idea for this game came to me while watching Countdown, thinking about how I could practice to improve my own scores. Word Blitz is a game where you receive a selection of letters and have to make words as fast as possible. Each time you submit a word you get a new selection of letters to make another word. Each word gives you a certain amount of points, longer words and words made quicker will award more points. The purpose of the game is to make words as fast as possible to earn a high score - great practice for Countdown.

At the moment I am currently working on implementing the Google API into my game which should allow players to earn achievements and post their scores to the leaderboards. It is not a difficult task, but since I have never published a game to the Play Store I am taking my time to learn exactly what I am doing. This part of the game should be done by tomorrow, and the main things left to do will be to fix a few bugs still in the game and some graphics required for when I publish it to the Play Store.

The game is almost done. One part I am still questioning whether to change is the time limit. The player starts with 60 seconds and can earn more time by making long words. With the current algorithm I use to award extra time I've managed to make a game go on for at least five minutes (it took many attempts though). I would like five minutes to be considered a very long game so I may have to tweak the algorithm to make sure that it is a hard task for the average player. The algorithm does stop the game going on for more than ten minutes for even the best of players so I have to be careful to keep that working - the best players will then be looking for long words too to get a high score.

If you are interested in playing the game (of course you are) then I will be posting another update next week where I will hopefully have a definite release date for my game. As I say, the game is almost complete, not long to wait now.
